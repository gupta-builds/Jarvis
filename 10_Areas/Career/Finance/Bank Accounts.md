A very clean system is:
- paycheck/income goes into **checking**
- automatic transfer goes from checking to **savings**
- fixed small automatic transfer goes from checking to **investing**
- all card payments are auto-paid from checking
Track only **5 buckets** every month:
- Fixed bills
- Food/groceries
- Transport
- Shopping/fun
- Savings/investing
Then do this:
- use **one main credit card** for most spending
- use checking only for rent or places that do not take cards
- set one day each month to review totals
- write down: income, total spent, total saved, total invested, card balance, checking balance, savings balance
Your monthly formula should be: **Income – bills – spending – savings – investing = leftover**
## Capital One Savor Student Credit Card
### Main card costs
- Purchase APR: **18.49%, 24.49%, or 28.49% variable**
- Cash advance APR: **28.49% variable**
- Annual fee: **$0**
- Late fee: **up to $40**
- Due date: **at least 25 days after the billing cycle closes**
### Best way to use this card
Use it for:
- dining
- entertainment
- streaming services
- normal grocery stores
Because the rewards in your terms are:
- **8%** on Capital One Entertainment ticketing purchases
- **5%** on hotels, vacation rentals, and rental cars booked through Capital One Travel
- **3%** on dining, qualified entertainment, popular streaming services, and grocery stores
- **1%** on everything else
### Very important catch
Your terms say grocery purchases at **gas stations, convenience stores, warehouse clubs, discount stores, and superstores** earn only **1%**, not 3%. That means stores like **Walmart** often won’t count as “grocery” for the better rate.
So for your setup:
- **Use OnePay** for the Walmart subscription benefit you mentioned
- **Use Savor Student** for dining, streaming, movies, concerts, and regular grocery stores
- **Do not use Savor for cash advances**
- **Do not use Savor for balance transfers unless you fully understand the fee/APR**
### When do you have to pay?
Your bill comes after the statement closes.  
The **due date is at least 25 days later**. If you pay the **statement balance in full by the due date**, you avoid interest on new purchases. If you do not, interest starts becoming a problem. Cash advances start charging interest immediately from the transaction date.
