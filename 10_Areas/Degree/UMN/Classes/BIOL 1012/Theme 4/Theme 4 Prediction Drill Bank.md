# Theme 4 Prediction Drill Bank

This is for active recall. Cover the answers, predict first, then check.

## Hormone Pathway Drills
### Drill 1: Low Testosterone
Prompt: A person with testes has abnormally low testosterone.

Prediction:
- GnRH increases.
- FSH increases.
- LH increases.
- Secondary sex characteristics may be less maintained.
- Sperm production may decrease unless the pathway restores testosterone.

Why: low testosterone removes negative feedback from hypothalamus/anterior pituitary.

### Drill 2: High External Testosterone
Prompt: A person takes testosterone supplements, causing high blood testosterone.

Prediction:
- GnRH decreases.
- FSH decreases.
- LH decreases.
- Leydig cell stimulation decreases.
- Sertoli support decreases.
- Sperm production can decrease, causing infertility risk.

Why: high testosterone suppresses upstream hormones by negative feedback.

### Drill 3: FSH Receptor Blocked in Sertoli Cells
Prompt: FSH is normal in blood, but Sertoli cells cannot respond.

Prediction:
- Sertoli support for spermatogenesis decreases.
- Sperm production decreases.
- Testosterone may remain normal if LH/Leydig pathway works.
- GnRH/LH may not change much unless testosterone changes.

Why: the hormone is present, but the target receptor pathway fails.

### Drill 4: LH Receptor Blocked in Leydig Cells
Prediction:
- Leydig cells produce less testosterone.
- Testosterone decreases.
- GnRH, FSH, and LH tend to increase because negative feedback drops.
- Sperm production decreases because testosterone support falls.

### Drill 5: GnRH Receptor on Pituitary Fails
Prediction:
- FSH decreases.
- LH decreases.
- Testes/ovaries receive weak stimulation.
- Testosterone or estrogen/progesterone decrease depending gonads.
- Gamete production and cycles are disrupted.

## Uterine/Ovarian Cycle Drills
### Drill 6: Day 3 of a 28-Day Cycle
Prediction:
- uterine phase = menses
- ovarian phase = early follicular
- endometrium is being shed/thin
- estrogen low
- progesterone low
- LH/FSH low-ish/basal

### Drill 7: Day 9 of a 28-Day Cycle
Prediction:
- uterine phase = proliferative
- ovarian phase = follicular
- endometrium getting thicker
- follicle growing
- estrogen increasing
- progesterone low

### Drill 8: Day 14 of a 28-Day Cycle
Prediction:
- ovulation
- LH surge
- FSH smaller surge
- estrogen high
- progesterone still low
- oocyte released from follicle

### Drill 9: Day 21 of a 28-Day Cycle
Prediction:
- uterine phase = secretory
- ovarian phase = luteal
- corpus luteum active
- progesterone high
- endometrium thick/oozy/maintained
- good implantation environment

### Drill 10: Progesterone Receptor Blocked in Uterus
Prompt: Progesterone is present, but uterine cells cannot respond.

Prediction:
- endometrium is not maintained normally
- menses may occur early
- secretory preparation fails

Why: target tissue needs the receptor response, not just hormone presence.

### Drill 11: Estrogen Cannot Positively Regulate Hypothalamus
Prediction:
- no strong GnRH increase
- no LH surge
- ovulation likely does not occur

Why: high sustained estrogen positive feedback triggers LH surge.

### Drill 12: Corpus Luteum Degenerates Early
Prediction:
- progesterone drops early
- endometrium breaks down
- menses/bleeding occurs early
- implantation support weakens

## Fertilization and Pregnancy Drills
### Drill 13: Fertilization at Ovulation
Prompt: Fertilization occurs on cycle day 14.

Prediction:
- fertilization location = fallopian tube/oviduct
- zygote forms day 0
- cleavage begins while embryo travels through tube
- morula forms before/around arrival at uterus
- blastocyst forms in uterus
- implantation around day 20-21

### Drill 14: hCG Is Not Produced
Prediction:
- corpus luteum degenerates
- progesterone drops
- endometrium is not maintained
- menses/pregnancy loss likely

### Drill 15: Hormonal Birth Control with Steady Estrogen/Progesterone
Prediction:
- GnRH/FSH/LH are suppressed/flattened
- no LH surge
- ovulation does not occur
- cervical mucus thickens if progestin effect present
- endometrium less favorable for implantation

### Drill 16: Need STI Prevention
Prediction:
- condoms/barrier methods are the answer
- hormonal methods prevent pregnancy but do not reliably prevent STI spread

## Cell Cycle and Cancer Drills
### Drill 21: DNA Damage Before S Phase
Prediction:
- G1 checkpoint should stop the cell before DNA is copied
- p53 is important here
- if not stopped, both daughter cells may inherit copied damage

### Drill 22: DNA Copying Error During S Phase
Prediction:
- G2 checkpoint should detect copied DNA problem
- if missed, one or both daughter cells may inherit mutation

### Drill 23: p53 Mutated
Prediction:
- damaged DNA cells are less likely to stop at G1
- mutations can be copied into daughter cells
- cancer risk increases

### Drill 24: Proto-Oncogene Becomes Oncogene
Prediction:
- growth signal becomes overactive
- cell divides more readily
- cancer risk rises, especially if tumor suppressors also fail

### Drill 25: BRCA Mutation Present
Prediction:
- DNA repair/tumor suppression is weakened
- breast/ovarian cancer risk increases
- cancer is not guaranteed
- additional mutations usually needed
- relatives may consider testing

### Drill 26: Chemotherapy Side Effects
Prediction:
- cancer cells affected because they divide rapidly
- hair follicles, gut lining, skin cells, and immune-related cells also affected
- side effects: hair loss, nausea/vomiting, fatigue, infection risk, bruising/bleeding

### Drill 27: PET Scan Bright Spots
Prediction:
- bright spots may be high glucose uptake
- cancer often bright because it is metabolically active
- brain and bladder can be bright for normal reasons
- distant abnormal bright spots suggest metastasis

## Ethics Drills
### Drill 28: Intersex Infant Surgery Without Medical Need
Autonomy:
- infant cannot consent; future person loses choice.

Beneficence:
- ask whether surgery improves health or mainly fits social expectations.

Nonmaleficence:
- unnecessary surgery can cause harm.

Justice:
- intersex traits should not be treated as shameful or less deserving of bodily autonomy.

### Drill 29: Puberty Blockers for a Transgender Adolescent
Autonomy:
- adolescent assent, guardian consent, and ongoing informed consent.

Beneficence:
- may reduce distress and buy time.

Nonmaleficence:
- weigh medication risks and harm of unwanted puberty.

Justice:
- access should not depend on state, cost, family resources, or clinic availability.

### Drill 30: AI Suggests a Cancer Treatment Plan
Autonomy:
- patient should know AI is involved.

Beneficence:
- AI could improve detection/planning.

Nonmaleficence:
- biased or unexplainable recommendations can harm.

Justice:
- benefits should not be available only to well-insured or wealthy patients.

## Rapid Mixed Questions
1. What hormone surge predicts ovulation?  
Answer: LH.

2. What hormone maintains the endometrium after ovulation?  
Answer: Progesterone.

3. What structure releases progesterone during the luteal phase?  
Answer: Corpus luteum.

4. What hormone maintains the corpus luteum in early pregnancy?  
Answer: hCG.

5. What produces hCG?  
Answer: Early embryo/trophoblast/chorion.

6. What is the usual location of fertilization?  
Answer: Fallopian tube/oviduct.

7. What is the usual location of implantation?  
Answer: Endometrium of uterus.

8. What cell-cycle phase copies DNA?  
Answer: S phase.

9. What checkpoint is p53 associated with?  
Answer: G1.

10. What is metastasis?  
Answer: Spread of cancer cells to distant tissues/organs.

11. What does AMH do?  
Answer: Causes Mullerian duct regression.

12. What do Wolffian ducts become with testosterone?  
Answer: Epididymis, vas deferens, seminal vesicles.

13. What do Mullerian ducts become without AMH?  
Answer: Fallopian tubes, uterus, cervix, upper vagina.

14. What prevents an embryo from being polyspermic?  
Answer: Egg membrane/zona reaction after first sperm entry blocks additional sperm.

15. What is the difference between morula and blastocyst?  
Answer: Morula is a solid ball; blastocyst is hollow with blastocoel, trophoblast, and inner cell mass.

16. What are the three germ layers?  
Answer: Ectoderm (skin + nervous system), mesoderm (muscle + bone + blood), endoderm (gut + respiratory lining).

17. When does differentiation begin in the embryo?  
Answer: Gastrulation (~week 3).

18. What is the corpus luteum → placenta transition?  
Answer: Weeks 1-10 corpus luteum makes progesterone (maintained by hCG); weeks 10-12+ placenta takes over.

19. Why does chemotherapy cause nausea and immune suppression?  
Answer: Gut lining cells and immune precursor cells divide rapidly, so chemo hits them too.

20. What is the difference between a benign and malignant tumor?  
Answer: Benign stays contained (no invasion); malignant invades surrounding tissue and can metastasize.

## Multi-Step Integration Drills

### Drill 31: Hormonal Birth Control → Why No Ovulation
Prompt: A person takes combined oral contraceptives (estrogen + progestin). Trace why ovulation does not occur.

Answer chain:
1. Synthetic hormones keep blood estrogen/progestin at steady medium level.
2. Steady estrogen provides constant negative feedback on hypothalamus.
3. GnRH release is suppressed/flattened.
4. Less GnRH → less FSH and LH from anterior pituitary.
5. No natural follicle-driven estrogen rise occurs (follicle growth is suppressed).
6. Without high sustained estrogen, no positive feedback switch.
7. Without positive feedback, no LH surge.
8. Without LH surge, no ovulation.
9. No egg released → fertilization impossible.

### Drill 32: What If Corpus Luteum Dies at Day 18?
Prompt: The corpus luteum degenerates abnormally early (day 18 instead of day 26-28).

Prediction:
- Progesterone drops early.
- Endometrium loses support → begins breaking down.
- Menses/bleeding starts early (short luteal phase).
- If fertilization occurred on day 14, the embryo would be only ~4 days old and may not have implanted yet.
- Without progesterone support, implantation fails or early pregnancy is lost.
- This is called "luteal phase defect" — a real cause of difficulty maintaining early pregnancy.

### Drill 33: Person Takes GnRH Agonist Continuously
Prompt: A person receives continuous GnRH agonist (like a puberty blocker or endometriosis treatment).

Prediction:
1. Initially, GnRH receptors on pituitary are stimulated → brief FSH/LH spike.
2. With continuous (non-pulsatile) exposure, pituitary GnRH receptors desensitize.
3. FSH and LH drop to very low levels.
4. Gonads receive no stimulation.
5. In person with testes: testosterone drops → puberty pauses, sperm production stops.
6. In person with ovaries: estrogen/progesterone drop → no follicle growth, no ovulation, no cycle.
7. Effects are reversible if agonist is stopped (receptors resensitize, normal pulsatile GnRH resumes).

### Drill 34: Both Copies of p53 Mutated
Prompt: A cell has lost both functional copies of p53.

Prediction:
- G1 checkpoint is severely weakened.
- DNA damage is not detected before S phase.
- Damaged DNA gets replicated.
- Daughter cells inherit mutations.
- Over time, additional mutations accumulate (in proto-oncogenes, other tumor suppressors).
- Cancer risk is very high.
- This is why p53 mutations are found in >50% of human cancers.

### Drill 35: BRCA1 Mutation + Oncogene Activation
Prompt: A person inherits one mutated BRCA1 allele. Later, the second BRCA1 copy is lost in a breast cell, AND a proto-oncogene becomes an oncogene in the same cell.

Prediction:
- BRCA1 loss → DNA repair is impaired (brake weakened).
- Oncogene activation → growth signals are overactive (gas pedal stuck).
- Both brakes and gas are compromised → high cancer risk in that cell lineage.
- This illustrates the "multiple hit" model — cancer usually needs several mutations.
- Not every cell with BRCA1 loss becomes cancerous, but adding an oncogene mutation dramatically increases the probability.

### Drill 36: Day 14 — Estrogen High But LH Receptors Blocked on Follicle
Prompt: Estrogen is high, LH surge occurs, but the mature follicle cannot respond to LH.

Prediction:
- LH surge happens (positive feedback worked).
- But follicle cannot rupture because it can't respond to LH signal.
- Ovulation fails.
- No corpus luteum forms.
- No progesterone rise.
- Endometrium stays in proliferative state (no secretory transformation).
- Eventually estrogen drops (follicle can't sustain itself indefinitely).
- Menses occurs, but cycle was anovulatory.

### Drill 37: Ectopic Pregnancy (Implantation in Fallopian Tube)
Prompt: A fertilized egg implants in the fallopian tube instead of the uterus.

Prediction:
- Embryo produces hCG → corpus luteum is maintained → progesterone stays high.
- Pregnancy test is positive.
- But fallopian tube cannot expand like the uterus.
- As embryo grows, tube can rupture → life-threatening internal bleeding.
- This is a medical emergency.
- Normal location of implantation = uterine endometrium. Anywhere else = ectopic.

### Drill 39: Cancer Staging and Treatment Selection
Prompt: Abby has ovarian cancer. PET scan shows bright spots only in the left ovary. No distant bright spots.

Prediction:
- Cancer appears localized (no metastasis visible).
- Surgery to remove the affected ovary/tube is likely first-line treatment.
- May preserve the other ovary (fertility consideration).
- Prognosis is better for localized vs metastatic cancer.
- If PET had shown distant bright spots → metastasis → systemic treatment (chemo) needed in addition to surgery.

### Drill 40: Ethical Scenario — Genetic Testing Reveals BRCA in Family
Prompt: A woman tests positive for BRCA1 mutation. Her sister has not been tested and does not want to know.

Ethical analysis:
- Autonomy: the sister has the right NOT to know (autonomy includes the right to refuse information).
- Beneficence: knowing could allow earlier screening and risk reduction.
- Nonmaleficence: forcing unwanted information can cause anxiety, affect insurance, change self-perception.
- Justice: both sisters should have equal access to testing and follow-up care if they choose it.
- Key point: one person's positive result reveals information about relatives who did not consent to testing.
