- LMX: leaders form different-quality relationships across followers (in-group/out-group).

## Lecture
- Leadership is a process: influence without needing positional power.
- Traits vs skills: traits don’t always lead to leadership; situation creates openings.
- Emergence vs effectiveness: political skill/self-promotion vs building teams/talent.
- Effective leadership behaviors map into task vs relationship orientation and the grid styles.
### Leadership as a process (not a role)
- Leadership = influencing others to mobilize and facilitate individual and collective efforts toward goals; you don’t need positional power for this.
- **Connection to textbook debates:** supports “born vs made” and “universal vs contingent” as open questions; influence can come from informal leaders, not only titles.
### Born vs made + traits/skills (lecture emphasis)
- Lecture separates **Traits (more innate)** vs **Skills (learned)** and lists examples: self-confidence and initiative/drive (traits), cognitive/technical/interpersonal (skills).
- Lecture also points out a limitation: having traits doesn’t guarantee leadership—situations create openings where leadership emerges.
### Emergence vs effectiveness (lecture distinction)
- **Emergence:** political skill, interpersonal savvy, effective self-promotion.
- **Effectiveness:** building high-performing organizations, cultivating talent, leading productive teams.
- **Connection to textbook:** helps explain why traits-based approaches alone are incomplete; follower perception + context shapes who “becomes” a leader vs who “is good.”
### Task vs relationship orientation (lecture examples + grid)
- Task-oriented: short-term planning, role clarity, monitoring performance.
- Relationship-oriented: supporting, developing, recognizing work.
- Managerial Grid styles shown in lecture (country club, impoverished, produce-or-perish, team leader), matching the textbook’s two-axis idea.

Traits vs skills in leadership (1 line each).::Traits are relatively stable personal characteristics; skills are learned capabilities like cognitive/technical/interpersonal.  
Emergence vs effectiveness (quick contrast).::Emergence is who gets seen as a leader; effectiveness is who actually builds performance and develops teams.  
Task-oriented leadership examples (3).::Short-term planning; clarifying roles/objectives; monitoring operations and performance.  
Relationship-oriented leadership examples (3).::Supporting subordinates; developing subordinates; recognizing work.  
What does the Managerial Grid measure?::Two dimensions: concern for production (task) and concern for people (relationship), producing distinct leadership styles.  
Transactional leadership (definition).::Leader–follower exchange where rewards/standards are used to get desired performance.  
Transformational leadership (definition).::Leader behaviors that create vision/meaning and stimulate followers to exceed expectations and improve outcomes.  
LMX theory (main claim).::Leaders develop different-quality relationships with different followers, forming in-groups and out-groups.
