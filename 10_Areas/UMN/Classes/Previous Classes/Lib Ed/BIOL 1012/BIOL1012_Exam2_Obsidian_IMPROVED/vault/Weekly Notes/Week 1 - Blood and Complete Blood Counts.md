## Key Points to Know

- **RBCs (erythrocytes):** transport oxygen via hemoglobin; low → fatigue, SOB, pallor.
- **WBCs (leukocytes):** immune defense; high → infection, leukemia, rejection, vaccine response.
- **Platelets (thrombocytes):** clotting; low → bleeding, bruising.
- **Plasma:** liquid portion of blood, includes clotting factors.
- **Serum:** plasma minus proteins; used for chemistry/biochemistry tests.
- **Hematocrit:** % of blood volume that is cells (mostly RBCs); low = anemia-like state.
- **Vitamin K deficiency:** reduces clotting *factors* (proteins), NOT platelet count.
- **Hemophilia:** low/nonfunctional clotting proteins, normal platelet count.
- **Sickle cell anemia:** RBCs abnormally shaped → poor O₂ transport.
- **Blood doping:** Darbopoietin (synthetic EPO) → more RBCs → more O₂ → unfair athletic advantage.

## CBC Patient Practice (from Lecture)

| Patient | Abnormal? | What it means |
|---------|-----------|---------------|
| 1 | Normal values | Could be: screening, pre-op, investigate fatigue/SOB/infection |
| 2 | **High WBC** | Infection, leukemia, organ rejection, vaccine response |
| 3 | **Very low platelets** | Thrombocytopenia, clotting disorder; predict bleeding |
| 4 | **Low RBC/hematocrit** | Sickle cell anemia; predict fatigue, poor O₂ delivery |

## Fast Recap

> RBC = oxygen transport | WBC = immune defense | Platelets = clotting  
> Plasma has clotting proteins; serum does not.  
> Hematocrit = % blood that is cells.  
> Vitamin K → clotting factors, not platelets.  
> Blood doping → high RBC, high hematocrit.
