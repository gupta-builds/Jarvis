## Key Points to Know

- **Innate = fast, broad, non-specific; Adaptive = slower first time, specific, has memory.**
- **Line 1:** Barriers (skin, mucus, tears, stomach acid, beneficial microbes).
- **Line 2:** Internal innate defenses (phagocytes, NK cells, complement, interferons, cytokines, inflammation, fever).
- **Line 3:** Adaptive immune system.
- **Phagocytes** (neutrophils, macrophages): engulf and destroy microbes; "Number Munchers."
- **NK cells:** detect missing HLA tags on infected/cancerous cells → kill them.
- **Complement proteins:** opsonize (coat), trigger inflammation, lyse (burst) microbes.
- **Interferons:** released by virus-infected cells; inhibit viral replication in nearby cells.
- **Cytokines:** signaling proteins that coordinate and amplify immune responses.
- **Inflammation:** capillaries become leaky → swelling, heat, redness, pain → leukocytes recruited.
- **Fever:** hypothalamus raises temp; hostile to pathogens; harmful if too high/prolonged.
- **Cytokine storm:** out-of-control systemic inflammation → ARDS, organ failure, death.
- **Vaccine side effects** = signs of innate immunity working (pain, fever, fatigue, swelling).

## Fast Recap

> Line 1 = keep out | Line 2 = broad attack | Line 3 = specific attack.  
> Phagocytes eat. NK cells kill suspicious cells. Complement coats, recruits, bursts.  
> Cytokines = signals. Interferons = antiviral. Inflammation = heat/red/swollen/pain.  
> Innate activates adaptive: phagocytes display antigens to T cells.
