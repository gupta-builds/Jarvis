# Chapter - 4
## 4.1
1. bulbourethral gland: secretion that cleanses the urethra prior to ejaculation 
2. clitoris: sensory structure in females; stimulated during sexual arousal 
3. external genitalia: reproductive structures visible on the outside of the body 
4. gametes: haploid cells that combine in fertilization to produce a new diploid individual; eggs and sperm 
5. gametogenesis: the process of egg or sperm development 
6. gonads: structures that produce gametes (eggs and sperm);female gonads are ovaries and male gonads are testes
7. internal genitalia all internal reproductive structures except for gonads 
8. interstitial cells of Leydig: cells in the testes that produce testosterone, also called Leydig cells 
9. labia majora: large folds of tissue covering the inguinal area 
10. labia minora: smaller folds of tissue within the labia majora 
11. oviduct: (also, fallopian tube) muscular tube connecting the uterus with the ovary area 
12. penis: male reproductive structure for urine elimination and copulation 
13. prostate gland: structure that is a mixture of smooth muscle and glandular material and that contributes to semen 
14. scrotum: sac containing testes; exterior to the body 
15. semen: fluid mixture of sperm and supporting materials 
16. seminal vesicle: secretory accessory gland in males; contributes to semen 
17. seminiferous tubule: site of sperm production in testes 
18. Sertoli cells: cells inside of the testes that protect sperm and help them to mature 
19. testes: pair of reproductive organs in males 
20. uterus: environment for developing embryo and fetus 
21. vagina: muscular tube for the passage of menstrual flow, copulation, and birth of offspring 
## 4.2
1. fertilization: the union of two haploid cells typically from two individual organisms 
2. karyotype: systematic arrangement of images of chromosomes into homologous pairs 
3. meiosis I: the first round of meiotic cell division; referred to as reduction division because the resulting cells are haploid 
4. meiosis II: the second round of meiotic cell division following meiosis I; sister chromatids are separated from each other, and the result is four unique haploid cells 
5. oogenesis: the process of producing haploid  eggs
6. reduction division: a nuclear division that produces daughter nuclei each having one-half as many chromosome sets as the parental nucleus; meiosis I is a reduction division 
7. somatic cell: all the cells of a multicellular organism except the gamete-forming cells  spermatogenesis the process of producing haploid sperm 
## 4.3
1. endocrine gland: an organ containing endocrine cells, cells that secrete hormones 
2. hormone: a chemical secreted by an endocrine gland into body fluids (usually blood) that affects the function of target cells 
3. hormone receptor: a molecule located on the surface of a cell, or inside it, that binds to a hormone; binding of a hormone to its receptor causes changes in the cell with the receptor 
4. stimulus: a signal that increases or decreases the level of hormone secreted by an endocrine cell 
5. target: a cell that expresses a hormone receptor, and changes its functions after the hormone binds to the receptor 
## 4.4
1. estrogen: reproductive hormone in females that assists in endometrial regrowth, ovulation, and calcium absorption 
2. follicle stimulating hormone (FSH): reproductive hormone that causes sperm production in men and follicle development in women 
3. gonadotropin-releasing hormone (GnRH): hormone from the hypothalamus that causes the release of FSH and LH from the anterior pituitary 
4. inhibin: hormone made by Sertoli cells; provides negative feedback to hypothalamus in control of FSH and GnRH release 
5. interstitial cell of Leydig: cell in seminiferous tubules that makes testosterone 
6. luteinizing hormone (LH): reproductive hormone in both men and women, causes testosterone production in men and ovulation and lactation in women 
7. menopause: loss of reproductive capacity in women due to decreased sensitivity of the ovaries to FSH and LH 
8. ovarian cycle: cycle of preparation of egg for ovulation and the conversion of the follicle to the corpus luteum 
9. ovulation: release of the egg by the most mature follicle 
10. progesterone: reproductive hormone in women; assists in endometrial re-growth and inhibition of FSH and LH release 
11. Sertoli cell: cell in seminiferous tubules that assists developing sperm and makes inhibin 
12. testosterone: reproductive hormone in men that assists in sperm production and promoting secondary sexual characteristics 
13. uterine cycle: cycle of the degradation and re-growth of the endometrium; also called the menstrual cycle
