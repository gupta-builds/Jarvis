## Key Cross-Chapter Connections

- Theory Y (Ch.1) + Relationship leadership (Ch.12) + Psychological safety (Ch.17) — all share the belief that trust and autonomy unlock human potential
- Big Five conscientiousness (Ch.13) + Task-oriented style (Ch.12) — high conscientiousness leaders gravitate toward task clarity and structure
- Internal locus of control (Ch.13) + Transformational leadership (Ch.12) — internals take initiative, making them more likely to lead change
- Culture (Ch.8) + Strategy implementation (Ch.5/6) — strategy without cultural alignment fails at execution
- Psychological safety (Ch.17) + EQ (Ch.12/13) — leaders build safety through self-awareness and emotional regulation
- Bounded rationality (Ch.15) + real decision-making — explains why excellent managers still make imperfect choices under constraint
- Escalation of commitment (Ch.15) + Corporate strategy (Ch.6) — bad bets persist because of sunk cost thinking
