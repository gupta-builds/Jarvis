# Hormones of Spermatogenesis

## Exam Objectives Covered
- HS1: identify source, stimulus, target, and response for GnRH, FSH, LH, testosterone.
- HS2-HS4: predict GnRH, FSH, LH, and testosterone changes under feedback changes.
- HS5-HS6: explain testosterone functions in sperm development and secondary sex characteristics.
- CR1: compare GnRH, LH, and FSH in persons with testes vs ovaries.

## 30-Second Version
Spermatogenesis needs a hormone cascade:

**Hypothalamus releases GnRH -> anterior pituitary releases FSH and LH -> testes respond.**

- FSH acts on **Sertoli cells** to support sperm production.
- LH acts on **Leydig cells** to produce testosterone.
- Testosterone supports sperm production, maintains reproductive tissues, drives many secondary sex characteristics, and negatively feeds back to the hypothalamus/pituitary.

## Core Anatomy
- **Testes**: gonads; contain seminiferous tubules, Sertoli cells, Leydig cells, and spermatogonia.
- **Seminiferous tubules**: location of sperm production.
- **Sertoli cells**: support developing sperm; respond to FSH.
- **Leydig cells**: secrete testosterone; respond to LH.
- **Spermatogonia**: diploid starting cells for sperm production.

Sperm path reminder: **seminiferous tubules -> epididymis -> vas deferens -> ejaculatory duct -> urethra -> penis**.

## Hormone Pathway Table
| Hormone | Main stimulus | Source | Target | Response/action |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| GnRH | Reproductive hormone regulation; low downstream gonadal hormone signal in feedback model | Hypothalamus | Anterior pituitary | Stimulates release of FSH and LH |
| FSH | GnRH from hypothalamus | Anterior pituitary | Sertoli cells in testes | Supports spermatogenesis |
| LH | GnRH from hypothalamus | Anterior pituitary | Leydig cells in testes | Stimulates testosterone secretion |
| Testosterone | LH stimulation of Leydig cells | Leydig cells in testes | Developing sperm/Sertoli environment, reproductive tissues, hypothalamus, anterior pituitary, secondary sex characteristic tissues | Supports sperm production, maintains reproductive structures, promotes secondary sex characteristics, inhibits GnRH/FSH/LH through negative feedback |

## Feedback Predictions
Negative feedback keeps hormone levels from drifting too far.

### If testosterone is too low
| Hormone/trait | Prediction | Why |
|---|---|---|
| GnRH | Increase | Less testosterone feedback inhibition on hypothalamus. |
| LH | Increase | More GnRH stimulates anterior pituitary. |
| FSH | Increase | More GnRH stimulates anterior pituitary. |
| Testosterone | Low until testes/Leydig response restores it | LH tries to push Leydig cells. |
| Secondary sex characteristics | Decrease/less maintained | Testosterone signal is lower. |
| Sperm production | Often decreases | Testosterone and FSH support sperm development. |

### If testosterone is too high
| Hormone/trait | Prediction | Why |
|---|---|---|
| GnRH | Decrease | High testosterone inhibits hypothalamus. |
| LH | Decrease | Less GnRH means less pituitary stimulation. |
| FSH | Decrease | Less GnRH means less pituitary stimulation. |
| Secondary sex characteristics | Increase/stronger maintenance | Testosterone signal is higher. |
| Sperm production | Can decrease if FSH/LH axis is suppressed too much | External/high testosterone can shut down upstream support. |

### If GnRH is too low
| Hormone | Prediction |
|---|---|
| FSH | Decrease |
| LH | Decrease |
| Testosterone | Decrease because Leydig cells get less LH |
| Sperm production | Decrease because Sertoli support and testosterone fall |

### If GnRH is too high
For this class, use the simple pathway logic unless the question says otherwise:

| Hormone | Prediction |
|---|---|
| FSH | Increase |
| LH | Increase |
| Testosterone | Increase through LH stimulation of Leydig cells |
| Feedback | Higher testosterone should then push GnRH/FSH/LH back down |

## Testosterone Effects
### In sperm development
- Supports spermatogenesis in the testes.
- Works with FSH/Sertoli cell support.
- Helps maintain adult reproductive function.

### In primary sex characteristic development
- During embryonic development, androgen signaling helps typical male reproductive structures develop.
- Development requires target tissues with working receptors.

### In secondary sex characteristics
Testosterone contributes to:
- facial/body hair growth
- voice changes
- increased muscle growth
- maintenance of adult reproductive tissues

Exam trap: testosterone is present in all adults at some level. The exam cares about relative level and receptor response.

## Common Disruption Scenarios
| Scenario | Best prediction |
|---|---|
| Damaged Sertoli cells with normal GnRH/FSH/LH | Low sperm count; hormone pathway can look normal but target cell support fails. |
| Damaged Leydig cells | Testosterone decreases; GnRH/LH/FSH tend to rise by reduced negative feedback. |
| Anterior pituitary cannot secrete FSH | Sperm production decreases because Sertoli cells are not stimulated. |
| Nonfunctional androgen receptor | Testosterone may be present, but target tissues cannot respond normally. |
| External testosterone is high | Upstream GnRH/LH/FSH can be suppressed by negative feedback. |

### Testosterone supplement trap
High external testosterone can lower sperm production:

1. blood testosterone is high
2. hypothalamus/pituitary reduce GnRH, FSH, and LH
3. Leydig cells receive less LH
4. Sertoli cells receive less FSH
5. spermatogenesis can drop even though testosterone in blood is high

This is why "more testosterone" does not automatically mean "more sperm."

### Inhibin backup detail
If inhibin appears in review/practice:

- source = Sertoli cells
- target = anterior pituitary
- action = suppresses FSH specifically
- job = feedback on sperm-production support

The provided 4.2 transcript emphasizes testosterone feedback more than inhibin, so prioritize testosterone unless a question names inhibin.

## Male vs Female Hormone Comparison
Same upstream names, different gonadal targets/responses.

| Hormone | In persons with testes | In persons with ovaries |
|---|---|---|
| GnRH | From hypothalamus; stimulates anterior pituitary to release FSH/LH | Same |
| FSH | Targets Sertoli cells; supports sperm production | Targets ovarian follicles; promotes follicle growth and estrogen secretion |
| LH | Targets Leydig cells; stimulates testosterone | Triggers ovulation and corpus luteum formation; supports progesterone/estrogen secretion |

The shared pattern is hypothalamus -> pituitary -> gonad. The target cells and final reproductive events differ.

## Exam-Style Applications
- Low testosterone in blood -> GnRH, LH, and FSH increase; secondary sex characteristics may decrease.
- High testosterone in blood -> GnRH, LH, and FSH decrease; secondary sex characteristics may increase.
- Low sperm count with normal GnRH, LH, and FSH -> target problem like damaged Sertoli cells.
- If LH is blocked -> Leydig cells are not stimulated -> testosterone falls.
