## Course map
MGMT 3001 is really one connected system:
- **Environment:** VUCA, globalization, industry forces, regulation, technology, and global differences shape what organizations face.
- **Strategy:** firms choose where to compete and how to create advantage.
- **Structure and scope:** organizations decide what to own, partner for, outsource, diversify into, or internationalize.
- **People:** leadership, teams, power, influence, conflict, and negotiation determine whether strategy becomes action.
- **Culture:** shared assumptions and values make behavior repeat without needing constant instruction.
- **Decision systems:** rational models help, but bounded rationality and bias explain why real choices go sideways.
- **Change:** organizations need dissatisfaction, a new model, and a process to move from old behavior to new behavior.
- **Ethics:** every system above can be used responsibly or badly. Ethics and CSR are not side topics; they shape trust, legitimacy, and long-term viability.
The entrepreneurship through-line is simple: a founder is not just building a product. A founder is building an organization that must choose a market, create advantage, hire people, form a culture, make decisions under uncertainty, manage conflict, use power responsibly, adapt to change, and operate inside a global/ethical environment.
> [!SUMMARY]
> The course map is strategy -> structure/scope -> people/teams -> culture -> decisions/change -> ethics/global environment. A good exam answer usually identifies where the problem sits in that system before choosing a framework.

## Highest-value frameworks
- **Management vs leadership:** order/consistency vs direction/alignment/motivation.
- **Contingency view:** fit the management approach to context.
- **Theory X/Theory Y:** assumptions about people shape systems and behavior.
- **Who/what/how strategy:** target customer, value offered, delivery method.
- **Five forces:** entrants, suppliers, buyers, substitutes, rivalry.
- **SWOT:** internal strengths/weaknesses plus external opportunities/threats.
- **Diversification test:** attractiveness, cost of entry, better-off.
- **Vertical integration tradeoff:** transaction costs vs administrative costs.
- **IPO framework:** inputs, processes/states, outputs.
- **Rational decision process:** define problem, criteria, weights, alternatives, evaluation, optimal choice.
- **Bounded rationality:** limited information, cognition, and time.
- **Culture levels:** artifacts, espoused values, underlying assumptions.
- **Change equation:** dissatisfaction x model x process > resistance + cost.
- **Power bases:** legitimate, reward, coercive, expert, referent.
- **Influence tactics:** rational persuasion, inspirational appeal, consultation, ingratiation, exchange, personal appeal, collaboration, apprising, coalition, pressure.
- **Conflict styles:** competing, collaborating, compromising, avoiding, accommodating.
- **Negotiation basics:** position, interest, BATNA, reservation value, bargaining zone.
- **Ethical frameworks:** utilitarianism, Kantianism, virtue, justice.
- **Triple bottom line:** profit, people, planet.
- **Global social dimensions:** power distance, individualism/collectivism, cooperative/competitive, uncertainty avoidance, long/short-term orientation.
> [!SUMMARY]
> These are the exam magnets. Know the definition, the mechanism, and the scenario trigger for each one.
