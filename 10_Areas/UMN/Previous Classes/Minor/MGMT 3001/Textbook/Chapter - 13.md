# Chapter 13 - Becoming a Leader
## Textbook anchor - leading yourself first
- ==Self-awareness is a leadership input: leaders need to understand their own traits, control beliefs, self-monitoring, and resilience before they can reliably lead others.==
- *Mechanism:* personality does not mechanically determine leadership, but it shapes default reactions under pressure. Self-awareness lets a manager adjust instead of acting on autopilot.
- Big Five use:
  - *Conscientiousness* supports reliability, planning, and follow-through.
  - *Extraversion* can help visibility and energy, but does not guarantee judgment.
  - *Agreeableness* can support relationships, but can make hard conversations harder if overdone.
  - *Emotional stability* helps under stress.
  - *Openness* supports learning and adaptation.
- Locus of control matters because it affects whether a manager responds to setbacks with agency or helplessness.
> [!SUMMARY]
> Chapter 13 is the bridge between leadership theory and personal practice: know your default pattern, then choose behavior that fits the situation.


## Becoming a leader: knowing yourself
- Chapter 13 supports the Week 2 leadership material by focusing on self-awareness.
- The chapter's core idea is that leadership effectiveness depends partly on understanding your own traits, assumptions, habits, and reactions.
- A leader who does not understand their own default behavior has a harder time adapting to followers and situations.

## Personality and leadership
- Personality traits are relatively stable tendencies that influence how people think, feel, and behave.
- The Big Five are the main personality framework used in the course:
  - Openness.
  - Conscientiousness.
  - Extraversion.
  - Agreeableness.
  - Neuroticism.
- Traits help explain tendencies, but they do not guarantee leadership effectiveness.

## Locus of control
- **Internal locus of control:** belief that outcomes are shaped substantially by one's own actions.
- **External locus of control:** belief that outcomes are shaped substantially by outside forces.
- This matters for management because locus of control affects initiative, accountability, and response to adversity.

## Self-monitoring and resilience
- **Self-monitoring:** reading cues from the environment and adjusting behavior accordingly.
- **Resilience:** ability to recover, adapt, and keep functioning through difficulty.
- These connect directly to leadership because managers need to regulate themselves before they can guide others.

## Managing difficult situations
- The chapter links self-awareness to difficult situations: stress, setbacks, conflict, ambiguity, and change.
- Leaders need to know how they react under pressure so they can respond deliberately instead of reflexively.
