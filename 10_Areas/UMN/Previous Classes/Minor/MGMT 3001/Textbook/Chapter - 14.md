# Chapter 14 - Power and Influence
## Textbook anchor - power and influence
- ==Power is the capacity to influence; influence is the process of changing thoughts, feelings, or behavior; authority is the formal right to influence.==
- *Mechanism:* managers rarely rely on one source of power. Formal authority, control of rewards, ability to punish, expertise, and referent appeal combine differently depending on the situation.
- Use power bases diagnostically:
  - legitimate power explains compliance with role authority,
  - reward and coercive power shape incentives and consequences,
  - expert power works when people trust knowledge,
  - referent power works when people identify with or admire the person.
- Influence tactics vary in effectiveness. Rational persuasion, inspirational appeal, consultation, collaboration, and apprising usually build more commitment than pressure or pure coalition tactics.
> [!WARNING]
> Authority can force compliance, but it does not automatically create commitment. For durable commitment, match the tactic to the person's interests, values, and information needs.


## Power and influence
- Power is the potential of one individual or group to influence another person's behavior, thinking, or attitudes.
- Influence is the means or vehicle by which power is exercised.
- Authority is formal legitimacy to influence.
- Leadership requires power, but power can be used effectively or misused.

## Why power matters
- Power helps leaders build coalitions around a vision or strategy.
- Power becomes more important when resources are scarce, decisions are uncertain, conflict is present, or stakeholders disagree.
- Not all powerful managers are influential. Influence requires using the right tactic with the right power base.

## Interpersonal power bases
- **Legitimate power:** formal position.
- **Reward power:** ability to give valued rewards or remove negatives.
- **Coercive power:** ability to punish.
- **Expert power:** knowledge, skill, or information.
- **Referent power:** liking, admiration, and personal identification.

## Influence tactics
- Rational persuasion.
- Inspirational appeal.
- Consultation.
- Ingratiation.
- Exchange.
- Personal appeal.
- Collaboration.
- Apprising.
- Coalition.
- Pressure.
