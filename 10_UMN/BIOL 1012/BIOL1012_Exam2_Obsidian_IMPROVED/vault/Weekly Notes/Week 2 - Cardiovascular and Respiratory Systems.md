## Key Points to Know

- **Cellular respiration equation:** Glucose + O₂ → H₂O + CO₂ + ATP
- **Primary function of cardiovascular system:** deliver O₂ and glucose to cells; remove CO₂.
- **Arteries = away from heart; Veins = toward heart** (direction, not oxygen content).
- **Right heart** → pulmonary circuit (to lungs, deoxygenated out).
- **Left heart** → systemic circuit (to body, oxygenated out).
- **Pulmonary artery = deoxygenated** (common exam trap!).
- **Pulmonary vein = oxygenated** (common exam trap!).
- Gas exchange occurs in **capillaries** (thin-walled, allow gas movement).

### Blood Flow Path (memorize!)
> Vena cava → Right atrium → Right ventricle → Pulmonary artery → **Lungs** → Pulmonary vein → Left atrium → Left ventricle → Aorta → Systemic arteries → Tissues → Systemic veins → Vena cava

### Inhalation Pathway (memorize!)
> Nose → Pharynx → Larynx → Trachea → Bronchi → Bronchioles → Alveoli

## Fast Recap

> Right → lungs → Left → body.  
> Artery = away. Vein = toward.  
> Pulmonary artery = deoxygenated (exception!). Pulmonary vein = oxygenated (exception!).  
> Gas exchange at capillaries: lungs (O₂ in, CO₂ out); tissues (O₂ out, CO₂ in).  
> Without circulation → cells run out of O₂/glucose → can die in minutes.
