## Key Points to Know

### Organ Donation Biology
- Rejection = adaptive immune system recognizing donor antigens as foreign → T cells and B cells attack the organ.
- **Match** = compatible ABO blood type + HLA antigens → lower rejection risk.
- **Anti-rejection drugs** = immunosuppressants given for life → reduce rejection but increase infection risk.
- WBCs removed from donated blood to prevent them from attacking the recipient.

### The Big Picture Connection
- **Central dogma** determines which antigen proteins cells make (DNA → RNA → protein).
- **Genetics** → unique antigen profile for each person.
- **Adaptive immunity** recognizes foreign antigens → can reject transplants.
- Transplantation sits at the intersection of genetics + blood biology + immunity + ethics.

### Ethics Case Study Highlights
| Case | Core Ethical Tension |
|------|---------------------|
| Non-paternity in donor testing | Autonomy (right to know) vs. Non-maleficence (harm of disclosure) |
| Felon seeking heart transplant | Justice (equal treatment) vs. questions about fairness to others on waiting list |
| Transplant tourism | Beneficence (care for patient) vs. Non-maleficence (supporting unethical systems) |

### Consent Types (exam-ready)
- **Informed consent:** participant understands study, risks, benefits before agreeing.
- **Specific consent:** data/tissue used only for specific question, time, researchers.
- **General consent:** broader permission for future unspecified use.

## Fast Recap

> Rejection = immune system correctly identifying foreign antigens on donor organ.  
> Match = compatible ABO + HLA → lower rejection risk.  
> Anti-rejection drugs: less rejection, more infection.  
> Four pillars: Autonomy, Beneficence, Non-maleficence, Justice.  
> Ethics = no single right answer — know how to make arguments from each pillar.
