A sensible beginner path is:
**Step 1:** Build emergency savings  
**Step 2:** Learn what stocks, ETFs, fees, diversification, and risk mean  
**Step 3:** Start small with automatic investing  
**Step 4:** Use broad, diversified funds rather than trying to guess winners
Investor.gov explains that diversification spreads risk across investments, and dollar-cost averaging is investing fixed amounts regularly over time. For a beginner, the cleanest long-term approach is usually:
- broad stock market index fund or ETF exposure
- automatic recurring investments
- hold for years, not days
- ignore short-term noise
That is much safer than “trading.”
