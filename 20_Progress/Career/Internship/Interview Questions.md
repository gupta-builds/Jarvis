# Questions
## Questions Asked in an Interview
1. *Why do you want to work here?*
	Best answer: “I ==admire== your company’s focus on innovation and customer experience. [MENTION PRODUCTS] After researching your recent projects and company culture, I’m excited by how my skills in [your field] align with your goals. I see this as an opportunity to contribute while continuing to grow in a ==forward-thinking environment==.”
2. *Why should we hire you?*
	Best answer: “I bring a ==blend of technical expertise==, problem-solving ability, and a ==proactive mindset==. I’ve successfully [mention a key achievement relevant to the role], and I’m confident I can deliver similar results while quickly adapting to your team’s processes.”.
3. *What are your strengths?*
	Best answer: “My strengths are adaptability, communication, and analytical thinking. [Also mention technical skills that align with the role]. For instance, I often take ==initiative== to find efficient solutions - such as [brief example]. These skills help me add value beyond my core responsibilities.”.
4. *What is your biggest weakness?*
	Best answer: “I tend to be very detail-oriented, which sometimes slows me down. But I’ve learned to balance ==accuracy with efficiency== by setting time limits for review and ==prioritizing== high-impact tasks.”
5. *Tell me about yourself.*
	Best answer: “I’m a [your role or degree] with experience in [field/industry]. Over the last few years, I’ve developed a passion for [specific skill/area], and I’m looking for a role where I can apply that passion to help drive results. [Share example]”
6. *What motivates you?*
	Best answer: “I’m motivated by challenges that allow me to grow and see tangible results. I enjoy solving complex problems and knowing that my work contributes directly to the team’s success.”
7. *Where do you see yourself in 5 years?*
	Best answer: “I aim to deepen my expertise in [your field] and take on leadership or specialized roles that allow me to mentor others and make a broader impact within the organization.”
8. *Describe a challenging situation and how you handled it.*
	Best answer: "In my previous project, we faced a tight deadline due to unexpected changes. I helped the team reorganize tasks, set priorities, and communicated progress clearly with stakeholders. We delivered on time and improved our workflow for future projects.”
9. *Tell me about a time you worked in a team.*
	Best answer: “I worked in a cross-functional team where clear communication was essential. I made sure to keep everyone aligned through updates and feedback, which helped us deliver a successful product ahead of schedule.”
10. *How do you handle criticism or feedback?*
	Best answer: “I value feedback as a chance to grow. When I receive constructive criticism, I take time to understand it fully and apply it immediately—this approach has helped me consistently improve.”
11. *What do you know about our company?*
	Best answer: “I know that your company is known for [specific achievements or values], and I’m particularly impressed by [recent initiative or innovation]. Your mission to [company goal] aligns with my own values and professional goals.”
12. *What are your salary expectations?*
	Best answer: “I’m open to discussing a fair compensation based on my skills, experience, and the responsibilities of this role. Could you share the range budgeted for this position?”
13. *How do you prioritize your work?*
	Best answer: “I start by identifying tasks with the most impact and closest deadlines. I use tools like task trackers to stay organized, and I revisit priorities daily to stay flexible for any new requirements.”
14. *How do you deal with stress or pressure?*
	Best answer: “I handle stress by staying organized and maintaining perspective. I break tasks into smaller steps and focus on one thing at a time, which keeps me productive even in high-pressure situations.”
15. *Why are you leaving your current job (or why did you leave your last job)?*
	Best answer: “I learned a lot in my previous role, but I’m looking for an opportunity that aligns more closely with my long-term career goals and allows me to take on new challenges.”
## Questions to ask the Interviewer
- How large is your team?
- What does your dev cycle look like? Do you do waterfall/sprints/agile?
- Are rushes to deadlines common? Or is there flexibility?
- How are decisions made in your team?
- How many meetings do you have per week?
- Do you feel your work environment helps you concentrate?
- What are you working on?
- What do you like about it?
- What is the work life like?
- How is the work/life balance?
