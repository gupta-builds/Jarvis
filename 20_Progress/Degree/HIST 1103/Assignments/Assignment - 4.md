Mathea Armstrong posted Jun 11, 2026 10:56 AM

When I looked at Vietnam and Indonesia, what stood out to me most was that independence did not automatically solve everything. Both countries had been controlled by European powers before becoming independent. Vietnam had been controlled by France, and Indonesia had been controlled by the Dutch. Because of that, both countries had to figure out how to become strong modern nations after years of outside control. From around 1960 to 2020, both Vietnam and Indonesia went through war, political conflict, economic struggles, and major changes in government.

For Vietnam, one major event was the Vietnam War and the fall of Saigon in 1975. Vietnam was divided between communist North Vietnam and anti-communist South Vietnam. The United States became involved because American leaders wanted to stop the spread of communism during the Cold War. This made Vietnam one of the biggest Cold War conflicts. The war caused massive destruction, including bombings, destroyed villages, deaths, and long-term damage from chemicals like Agent Orange. When Saigon fell in April 1975, the war ended and Vietnam was reunited under communist control. In 1976, Vietnam became the Socialist Republic of Vietnam. I think this event is important because Vietnam became united, but only after a huge amount of suffering.

A second important event for Vietnam was the Doi Moi reforms that began in 1986. After the war, Vietnam struggled with poverty and shortages. The strict communist planned economy was not working well, so the government allowed more private business, trade, and foreign investment. The Communist Party still controlled politics, but the economy became more open. This helped Vietnam grow and become more connected to the world. By the 1990s and 2000s, Vietnam became known for manufacturing and exports. To me, this shows that Vietnam found a way to modernize economically without changing its whole political system.

For Indonesia, one important person was Sukarno, the country’s first president. Sukarno helped lead Indonesia after independence from the Dutch. He wanted the country to be strong and united, but that was difficult because Indonesia has many islands, cultures, religions, and ethnic groups. By the 1960s, Indonesia had serious political and economic problems. Sukarno created “Guided Democracy,” where he held more power and tried to balance the military, nationalists, religious groups, and communists. I think Sukarno shows how hard it was for a new country to stay united after colonial rule.

A second major event for Indonesia was the rise of Suharto after 1965. After an attempted coup, the military blamed the Communist Party, and mass killings followed. Hundreds of thousands of suspected communists and left-wing supporters were killed. Suharto then took power and created the New Order government. His rule brought economic growth and development, but it also limited political freedom and had serious corruption. In 1998, Suharto resigned after protests and an economic crisis, which helped Indonesia move toward democracy.

Overall, Vietnam and Indonesia both show that becoming a modern nation after colonialism was not simple. Vietnam’s path included war and economic reform, while Indonesia’s path included Sukarno’s leadership, Suharto’s dictatorship, and later democracy. Both countries changed a lot by 2020, but they had to go through violence, instability, and rebuilding first.

My question: Do you think economic growth or political freedom is more important when a country is trying to become stable after colonialism?
