# Discussion
## Pre-work
1. Assignment (required to upload to dropbox as well as posted here in the discussion section) due date for assignments each week is Saturday with the answer to the classmate questions (not required to upload to dropbox, just post as a reply here in the discussion section) due date is Sunday, both at 11:30pm.  You must to submit your answer to the assignment questions to the dropbox AND the discussion section.  Don't forget to ask a question of another student, and answer another student's question for EACH assignment.
2. Minimum word count for assignments is 400 words. 
3. Turnitin similarity score on your assignments submitted to the dropbox is a max of 15%.
4. Ask a question at the end of each assignment that is relevant to your answer and the topic.
5. For responses to student questions, target about 100 words but less or more is ok as there is no actual minimum.
6. Post here, and upload to the dropbox (Assignments tab, then the correct assignment number) as well.
7. Make a good faith effort to answer the questions I post as assignments.  Always use data and details to prove your points.  Remember, you are trying to convince the reader that what you are saying is correct.  Facts are needed for that. avoid such nonsense as, "On page 43, Jones says blah blah blah"  That is really just padding the word count and is best avoided.
8.  When writing your answer, be sure to include one quote from your textbook in your answer. 
It does not need to be long, but one quote is required. All parts must be completed for credit.  There is no partial credit.  Determining credit is simple.  If you followed all the requirements in this list, you have credit.  If you did not, you don't.
## The Prompt
- Hello everyone.

This topic has polarized and confused many people for decades, so if you don't feel like you have a good handle on it, that is actually a good thing!  It is vast and complex, made much more so by the recent events in which the US and Israel unilaterally attacked Iran, and Iran has responded by attacking US and Israeli interests throughout the Middle East (so far).  I certainly did not plan for this chapter and the events to coincide as you can see from the chapters listed far ahead of today in the syllabus.  However, it gives us a unique opportunity to talk about such a complex subject; remember, this is a history class.  I expect each of you to respond as a historian would, critically thinking and looking at multiple perspectives and a wide range of sources when developing your answers.  Here we go.

This assignment follows the path of most of our assignments so far.  All peoples that live in the region have a long history in it.  Having a long history with a region does not, in this modern world, preclude an inalienable right to be there.  If that were the case, the country of Greece could claim not just Greece, but much of southern Italy, Southern France, Southeast Spain, parts of Iraq, Syria, Iran, Afghanistan, India, and most of the coastline of the Black Sea (including the Crimean Peninsula) simply by founding long-standing colonies there 2500 years ago.  Historically, this argument is a non-starter.  We need to look at the contemporary world, post 1945.

From 1945, this region has been beset by violence.  Putting aside any historical claims to rights of ownership (which we have already shown are irrelevant),  
(Part 1.) what are 2 issues (one from an Arab perspective and one from an Israeli perspective) that are causing the heart of the disagreement today?   
  
Look deeply, and be specific.  (Hint: they are **_not_** innate animosity toward each others religions).  Keeping in mind the history of the region for the 19th and early 20th centuries, clearly describe the basis for each argument.  
  
Part 2.  Choose one of the following: (the 1948 War, The Suez Canal Crisis of 1956, The 6 Day War (1967) and 1973 War), and discuss it's general causes from again, both sides of the conflict (be sure to mention what nations were involved).  
  
Part  3.  
There have been several peace efforts since the 1980s.  Almost every US administration has attempted to encourage peace in the region since 1992.  Pick one attempt, list it's goals and whether or not a treaty was actually signed.  What made those treaties fail, or the process fall apart?  (provide some evidence for your claims).

Part 4.  Look at the entire Middle East (from Egypt to Iran, but not Turkey for this discussion) over the last 20 years down to today.  Briefly list 4 contemporary events during the period (they do not have to be conflicts; they may also be achievements).  How does the event you have chosen contribute to either stabilization of the region, or destabilization, and why do you think so?

When you have finished, please go to the "Questions and Other Info" forum and the subtopic "Questions" below and post a question about current  events.  Each person posting a legitimate question and engaging in the ensuing discussion (you are all encouraged to respond) will receive one extra credit point to be added on to your grade at the end of the semester.  
  
Good luck!  Let me know if you have questions.  This is a long assignment, so be prepared to go well over the word count minimum.

Dr. Homann
