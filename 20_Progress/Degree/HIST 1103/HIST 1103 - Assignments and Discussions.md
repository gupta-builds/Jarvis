# HIST 1103 — Assignments and Discussions
Assignments and participation are **20% of the grade**. Every assignment lives in two places at once: a **post in the Discussions tab** and an **uploaded file in the Assignments tab dropbox**. Both are required, every time. This note merges the syllabus rules with the live D2L discussion-board and dropbox instructions.
## The basic loop for each assignment
1. The instructor posts the question(s) in the Discussions tab under "Weekly Assignments."
2. You write your answer (minimum 400 words).
3. You **post the answer as a reply** in the Discussion section (no attachments) **and upload the same file to the matching dropbox** in the Assignments tab.
4. You **end your answer with a question** about the topic for a classmate.
5. You **reply to at least one other student's question** at the end of their post.
6. You keep your **Turnitin similarity at 15% or less**.
## Credit rules (pass/fail, no partial credit)
- Assignments are graded **pass/fail** (credit / no credit), not with letter grades. There is **no partial credit**: if you followed every requirement you get credit; if you missed any, you do not.
- There are **13 assignments** plus **1 practice assignment** (the practice is ungraded but **required**). The syllabus is internally inconsistent here: one line says "There will be 13 Assignments," another says "one ungraded (but REQUIRED) practice assignment and **twelve** assignments counted." The schedule lists Assignments 1–13, so treat it as **13 graded assignments + 1 practice** and confirm with the instructor.
- Missing the practice assignment = failing a course requirement = an **automatic one-letter-grade drop** in your final grade.
- The syllabus states the credit math two ways (record both, confirm with instructor):
	- *Threshold to pass:* you must turn in **7 of the 13** assignments (plus the practice) to possibly pass. Anyone completing **6 or fewer** for full credit may not pass.
	- *Full-credit points (200 total):* completing **10 of 13** for full credit = all 200/200 points. Each full-credit assignment missing below 10 cuts the score by 75 points cumulatively → **9 = 125, 8 = 50, 7 = 0**.
	- *Also stated:* you must get **full credit on at least 8 assignments** to pass; you can have up to 5 without full credit and still possibly pass, though that earns **zero points** for the assignment portion.
- Assignments that do not meet **all** criteria do **not** count toward the "full credit" total.
- "Full credit" = turned in **and** met every requirement. You can turn one in late, not earn full credit, yet still have it count toward the 7-needed threshold (but it earns zero points).
## The 7 acceptance criteria (must hit all)
1. **Minimum 400 words** (this is a floor, not a target — going well over is fine; the instructor counts words).
2. **On time** (late = no points) to **both** the Assignment-tab dropbox **and** the Discussion-section post.
3. **Good-faith effort:** good grammar and proper citation of sources. Cite using CMOS (Chicago Manual of Style): https://owl.purdue.edu/owl/research_and_citation/chicago_manual_17th_edition/cmos_formatting_and_style_guide/general_format.html
4. **Ask a question** about the topic at the end of your assignment for a classmate to answer.
5. **Answer (reply to) at least one** other student's end-of-assignment question.
6. **Turnitin similarity 15% or less.**
7. **Include at least one quote with its page number** from the textbook; the quote does **not** count toward your 400-word minimum.
## Discussion-board rules (from the "Weekly Assignments" forum)
- Post each assignment in the discussion **and** upload it to the correct dropbox.
- Watch due dates and word minimums; always include a question at the end and answer a classmate's question.
- **Assignment due:** Saturday at 11:30 PM (posted to the discussion **and** the dropbox).
- **Reply to a classmate's question due:** Sunday at 11:30 PM (discussion only — not uploaded to the dropbox).
- Minimum word count for assignments: **400 words**.
- Turnitin on dropbox submissions: max **15%**.
- The end-of-assignment question must be relevant to your answer and the topic.
- Replies to a student's question: aim for about **100 words**, but there is no real minimum.
- Use **data and details** to prove your points — you are convincing the reader, so facts matter. Do **not** pad with filler like "On page 43, Jones says blah blah blah."
- Include **one quote** from the textbook in your answer (it does not need to be long, but one is required).
- All parts must be done for credit; there is no partial credit.
- Want individual feedback? Email the instructor, drop by his online Zoom office hours, or use the in-course messaging envelope icon.
## How to post an assignment (step by step from the syllabus)
1. Go to the **Discussions** tab from the course home page.
2. Read the requirements under "Weekly Assignments," then scroll to that week's assignment.
3. Click the instructor's assignment post for that week — the text appears in a box at the top. Click **Reply** (top-left of that box), type your answer, then click **Submit** (bottom-right). It posts automatically in the right spot. **Do not attach documents to the post.**
4. Go to the **Assignments** tab and find the dropbox with the same name. Upload your file (check the extension: .docx, .doc, .rtf, or .pdf).
5. After uploading, click **"1 Submission/1File"** to see your Turnitin similarity score. If it is over 15%, fix the essay until it is 15% or below — a submission scored above 15% is **not accepted**. Suspected plagiarism = immediate course failure and a referral to the Dean.
6. Go back and read everyone else's posts, and reply to a classmate's question using the same Reply method on their individual post (so the reply lands with the correct post).

## When assignments are posted
- Each week's assignments (usually 4) are posted Monday, Tuesday, Wednesday, and Thursday by noon each day.
- One assignment is posted after the Midterm, during the week the Midterm is assigned.
- The instructor may post assignments early; that does **not** change the due dates.
- He may occasionally move a due date — if so, he states it in the assignment post or in the "Information" discussion area. Otherwise, all due dates are as listed in the syllabus.
