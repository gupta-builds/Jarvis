# HIST 1103 — Exams
There are two exams: a **Midterm** and a **comprehensive Final**. Together they are **80% of the course grade** (Midterm 30%, Final 50%). You **must take both exams to pass**. Both are written as essay/position papers and submitted only to the dropbox (never posted in the discussion).
## Format and word minimums
- **Midterm:** position-paper format, **1250 (minimum) to 3000+ words**.
- **Final:** comprehensive essay, **1750 to 3000+ words**.
- Rough conversion: about **250 words per typed page**.
- Only the **word count of your actual answer** counts toward the minimum.
- **Bibliographies are required** but do **not** count toward the word total.
- Missing the word minimum = an automatic **"F" on that exam**.
- Keep your **Turnitin similarity at 15% or less**.
## Timing
- Exam questions are posted by the instructor on **Monday of the exam week by noon**.
- Each exam is due by **11:30 PM on Thursday of the same week**.
- **Midterm due: Thursday, June 18, 11:30 PM.**
- **Final Exam due: Thursday, July 2, 11:30 PM.** No late finals are accepted.
- There are no make-up exams or alternate completion times without the instructor's prior discussion and approval.

## Late exam penalties
The syllabus describes the late penalty two ways — both are recorded here; confirm which applies.
- *Exam section wording:* lateness is counted in whole days (11:31 PM to 24 hours late = 1 day; 24.1–48 hours = 2 days; and so on). Grade is reduced **one full letter grade per day late**.
- *Grading-policy wording:* late exams lose **10 percentage points per day late**, plus **10 percentage points for every 5% above the 15% Turnitin limit**. Example: 3 days late = 30 points off, so the max becomes 70 (a 95 → 65). Points are deducted after scoring.
- A submission in an **unacceptable file format** is treated as **not submitted** — only use .pdf, .rtf, .doc, or .docx.
- Always submit even if late — any credit beats zero.
## Follow-up meetings
For any assignment or exam, the instructor may ask to follow up over a Zoom (or in-person) meeting to ask you about your study, research, and writing process. If he requests this, your grade is **delayed until you meet**, and the meeting must happen **within one week** of grades being returned to still earn a passing grade.
## Where grades appear
Exam grades are returned only in the **"Feedback"** area of the dropbox submission — nowhere else — so you must open the feedback to see your grade.
## Plagiarism reminder
Write in your own words; do not just rearrange a source's words without citing. No more than 15% of your writing may come from other sources. Everything is checked by Turnitin in D2L. Suspected plagiarism = immediate course failure.
