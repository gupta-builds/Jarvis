# Mentor Meeting — Hackathons, Summer, and Networking
## Meeting Focus
Three things I want from this conversation with [[Mentor Details|Ahnaf]]:
1. Which hackathon project is the stronger portfolio artifact, and how should I frame it?
2. What should I focus on this summer if I do not have an internship?
3. How do I start networking more intentionally — especially across Best Buy?
This meeting builds on the broader [[Plan]] but focuses specifically on turning recent hackathon work into career momentum.
## What I Have Done Recently
I participated in two hackathons around [[01_Summit|O1 Summit 2026]]:
- **[[Opspilot]]** — AI restaurant operations dashboard. Core flow: reservation → invoice → payment → finance → feedback → recovery action. Deterministic services owned money; AI handled communication and prioritization.
- **[[Resq]]** — Fintech pivot from OpsPilot. CFO cash survival workspace: cash truth from cleared ledger facts, 13-week deterministic forecast, breakpoint detection, ranked action queue. AI advises but never touches the numbers.
Both projects are documented with architecture details, what was real vs mocked, and honest assessments of what went wrong. See the individual notes for full technical breakdowns.
### What I took away from both builds
- I can build more than toy apps when I have a clear workflow and time pressure.
- AI projects are stronger when deterministic systems protect the important truth.
- Hackathon scope gets out of control fast — one hero flow beats feature breadth.
- A polished pitch matters as much as the code.
- If I do not document what was real vs mocked, I will struggle to explain the project later.
## Current Direction
I want to get better at turning work into stories — portfolio pages, resume bullets, networking explanations, interview answers. Right now I am interested in:
- Networking more intentionally.
- Improving communication and pitching.
- Building more projects with portfolio-grade polish.
- Exploring AI application engineering.
- Possibly research, but not making it the main focus.
## What I Want From Ahnaf
Specific asks:
- **Project feedback:** Which project — [[Opspilot]] or [[Resq]] — sounds more impressive to an engineering manager, and why?
- **Framing help:** How to write resume bullets and portfolio descriptions that are honest about what was real vs mocked without underselling the work.
- **Summer focus:** If no internship, what combination of project-building, system design, AI/ML, and networking makes the summer look productive and credible?
- **Networking:** Introductions to people in different engineering/product roles at Best Buy, if appropriate. How to approach people without sounding like I am just asking for referrals.
- **Architecture feedback:** Does the service-layer separation (routes → services → domain → queries) read as mature for a student project?
- **Pitch feedback:** How to explain technical products clearly to engineers, managers, and non-technical people.

## Meeting Flow
In-hand version: [[Mentor Meeting - One-Pager]]. Do not try to cover everything. Run it in five parts.
### Part 1 — Frame (~1 min)
"I wanted to use this meeting to get your advice on turning my recent hackathon work into something career-useful. I worked on two projects at O1 Summit: OpsPilot and Resq. I want your help on three things: which project is the stronger portfolio artifact, how I should shape my summer if I do not have an internship, and how I can start networking more intentionally."
### Part 2 — Recent work (~2 min)
45 seconds on OpsPilot:
"OpsPilot was an AI operations dashboard for a restaurant manager. The best part was the connected workflow: reservation, invoice, payment, finance update, feedback analysis, and recovery action. The biggest technical lesson was that AI became useful only after the deterministic workflow was solid."

45 seconds on Resq:
"Resq was the sharper pivot. It became a CFO-style cash workspace: cash truth, 13-week forecast, breakpoint detection, and ranked actions. The strongest idea was keeping AI advisory-only and not letting it touch the numbers."

30 seconds on what went wrong across both:
"In both projects, the hardest part was not coding alone. It was scope control, demo truth, and explaining clearly what was real versus mocked."
### Part 3 — Main decision (~5 min)
Lead with the highest-value question:
"Between OpsPilot and Resq, which one sounds more impressive to an engineering manager, and why?"

Follow up depending on the answer:
- If he says Resq → ask how much more polish it needs before it becomes a flagship case study.
- If he says OpsPilot → ask whether to narrow it to one workflow and polish that.
- If he says both have value → ask which should be the main project and which stays as a supporting artifact.

Then: "How should I describe what was real vs mocked without underselling the work?"
### Part 4 — Summer planning (~5 min)
Use the summer options table from this note as a discussion device, not something to read aloud.

"If I do not have an internship, I do not want to waste the summer bouncing between random tutorials. I want to choose one or two priorities that make the summer look credible. Right now I see five buckets: system design, AI/ML, research, project-building, and networking/communication. Which one or two would you weight heaviest for someone in my position?"

Follow-up: "Would you rather see me go deeper on one flagship project, or build several smaller ones?"

If time: "How much system design should I learn before internship interviews?"
### Part 5 — Networking + concrete ask (~3 min)
Save networking for the end so it feels grounded in the work just discussed.

"I also want to get better at networking intentionally, not just asking people for referrals. If I want to learn more about product-facing engineering teams like BestBuy.com or mobile, which roles should I try to talk to first?"

One concrete ask: "If it feels appropriate, would you be open to introducing me to one frontend engineer and one backend engineer for informational conversations later on?"

Close: "So my main next step is ___, and I will update my notes and plan after this."
