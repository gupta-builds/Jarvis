# Cardiovascular and Respiratory Systems

## CV 1 — Pulmonary vs. Systemic Circuits

### Pulmonary Circuit
- Pathway: **Heart ↔ Lungs**
- Function: carry **deoxygenated** blood from heart to lungs for gas exchange; return **oxygenated** blood to heart.
- Key vessels: **pulmonary artery** (heart → lungs), **pulmonary vein** (lungs → heart).

### Systemic Circuit
- Pathway: **Heart ↔ Rest of Body**
- Function: deliver **oxygen and glucose** to all tissues; carry **carbon dioxide and waste** back to heart.
- Key vessels: **aorta** (heart → body), **vena cava** (body → heart).

---

## CV 2 — Trace the Path of Blood

### Full Loop (memorize this order)
1. Body tissues
2. Systemic veins
3. **Vena cava** (superior or inferior)
4. **Right atrium**
5. **Right ventricle**
6. **Pulmonary artery** ← deoxygenated blood leaves right heart
7. **Lungs / pulmonary capillaries** ← gas exchange happens here
8. **Pulmonary vein** ← oxygenated blood returns
9. **Left atrium**
10. **Left ventricle**
11. **Aorta** ← oxygenated blood leaves left heart
12. Systemic arteries
13. Systemic capillaries ← gas exchange happens here
14. Systemic veins → back to vena cava

### Exam-Critical Example: Biceps → Spleen
> Biceps → **brachial vein** → **vena cava** → **right atrium** → **right ventricle** → **pulmonary artery** → **lungs** → **pulmonary vein** → **left atrium** → **left ventricle** → **aorta** → **splenic artery** → **spleen**

### Artery vs. Vein Rule
- **Arteries carry blood AWAY from the heart.**
- **Veins carry blood TOWARD the heart.**
- This is about direction, not oxygen content!

### Organ-Specific Vessel Naming
- Vessels named for the organ they serve: brachial artery/vein (biceps), renal artery/vein (kidneys), splenic artery/vein (spleen).

## CV 3 — Oxygenated vs. Deoxygenated Blood

| Oxygenated (Red) | Deoxygenated (Blue) |
|-----------------|---------------------|
| Pulmonary veins | Pulmonary artery |
| Left atrium | Right atrium |
| Left ventricle | Right ventricle |
| Aorta | Vena cava |
| All systemic arteries | All systemic veins |

> **Common Exam Trap:** The **pulmonary artery** is an artery carrying **deoxygenated** blood. The **pulmonary vein** is a vein carrying **oxygenated** blood. Vessels are named by *direction from the heart*, not by oxygen content.

### Which side of the heart does what?
- **Right heart** → pumps deoxygenated blood to lungs (pulmonary circuit).
- **Left heart** → pumps oxygenated blood to body (systemic circuit).

### Heart chamber roles
- **Atria** receive incoming blood.
- **Ventricles** pump blood out.

## CV 4 — Cellular Respiration and Why Blood Flow Matters

### Cellular Respiration Equation
$$\text{Glucose} + \text{Oxygen} \rightarrow \text{Water} + \text{Carbon Dioxide} + \text{ATP (energy)}$$

- **Glucose + O₂ → H₂O + CO₂ + ATP**
- This happens in the **mitochondria** of cells.

### Why every tissue needs blood flow
- Cells need a **continuous supply of O₂ and glucose** from the blood.
- Without circulation, cells stop making ATP and begin to fail within **minutes**.
- Blood also removes **CO₂** (waste) from tissues.

### Primary and secondary functions of the cardiovascular system
- **Primary:** deliver O₂ and glucose to cells; remove CO₂.
- **Secondary:** transport nutrients, hormones, salts, wastes, immune cells, platelets.

## Respiratory 1 — Cellular Respiration and Breathing

- **Breathing** brings O₂ into the body and expels CO₂.
- O₂ is a **reactant** in cellular respiration.
- CO₂ is a **product** of cellular respiration.
- Equation again: **Glucose + O₂ → H₂O + CO₂ + ATP**

---

## Respiratory 2 — Cardiovascular and Respiratory Systems Working Together

- **Respiratory system:** moves air into lungs; provides the gas exchange interface.
- **Cardiovascular system:** moves blood through lungs (picks up O₂, drops off CO₂); distributes O₂ to tissues; returns CO₂ to lungs.
- Together they function as a **delivery-and-removal system** for metabolic gases.

---

## Respiratory 3 — Gas Exchange

### Where gas exchange happens
- In **capillaries** — tiny, thin-walled blood vessels.
- Two locations:
  - **Pulmonary capillaries** (lungs, near alveoli)
  - **Systemic capillaries** (all other tissues)

### Pulmonary capillaries (in the lungs, at the alveoli)
- **O₂ moves from alveolar air → INTO blood**
- **CO₂ moves from blood → OUT into alveolar air**
- Blood becomes oxygenated here.

### Systemic capillaries (at tissues)
- **O₂ moves from blood → OUT into tissue cells**
- **CO₂ moves from tissue cells → INTO blood**
- Blood becomes deoxygenated here.

### Connecting to oxygenated/deoxygenated blood
- After **pulmonary capillaries** → blood is oxygenated → pulmonary vein → left heart.
- After **systemic capillaries** → blood is deoxygenated → systemic vein → vena cava → right heart.

## Respiratory 4 — Order of Structures When Breathing In

**Air inhalation pathway (in order):**

> Nose/Nasal cavity → **Pharynx** → **Larynx** → **Trachea** → **Bronchi** → **Bronchioles** → **Alveoli** (in lungs)

**Exhalation:** same structures in **reverse order**.

> Mnemonic: **Please Let The Big Brown Animal Live** = Pharynx, Larynx, Trachea, Bronchi, Bronchioles, Alveoli, Lungs

## Summary Comparison Table

| Feature | Pulmonary Circuit | Systemic Circuit |
|---------|------------------|-----------------|
| Route | Heart ↔ Lungs | Heart ↔ All body tissues |
| Main job | Oxygenate blood, remove CO₂ | Deliver O₂/glucose, remove CO₂/waste |
| Starting vessel | Pulmonary artery | Aorta |
| Returning vessel | Pulmonary vein | Vena cava |
| Heart side | Right heart pumps out | Left heart pumps out |

## Memory Anchors
- **Right heart → Lungs → Left heart → Body**
- **Artery = Away from heart**
- **Vein = Toward heart**
- **Pulmonary artery = deoxygenated** (exception to "arteries carry red blood")
- **Pulmonary vein = oxygenated** (exception to "veins carry blue blood")
- **Gas exchange = capillaries**
- **Lungs: O₂ in, CO₂ out | Tissues: O₂ out, CO₂ in**
