## Key Points to Know

- **Antigen:** small, specific molecular feature on a pathogen or foreign cell that triggers adaptive immunity.
- **BCR and TCR:** receptor proteins on cell membranes; have variable (antigen-specific), constant, and transmembrane regions.
- **Antibodies:** secreted by B cells; same specificity as BCR; travel in body fluids; neutralize, opsonize, activate complement.
- **B cells:** best against extracellular bacteria; secrete antibodies.
- **Helper T cells:** coordinate by secreting cytokines; DO NOT directly kill; HIV targets these.
- **Cytotoxic T cells:** inject toxic granules to kill infected/cancerous cells; best against viruses.

### The 4 Steps (for all lymphocytes):
1. **Encounter** — receptor binds matching antigen.
2. **Activate** — clonal expansion: thousands of identical copies made.
3. **Attack** — effector cells fight the pathogen (antibodies, cytokines, or direct killing).
4. **Memory** — long-lived memory cells persist for future encounters.

- **High WBC on CBC** = sign that clonal expansion of adaptive immune cells is occurring.
- **Antigen-antibody complex** activates complement → triggers phagocytosis, inflammation, AND cytolysis.

## Fast Recap

> B cells → antibodies → best for bacteria (extracellular).  
> Helper T → cytokines → coordinate everything.  
> Cytotoxic T → kill infected cells → best for viruses (intracellular).  
> 4 steps: Encounter, Activate (clone!), Attack, Memory.  
> HIV depletes helper T → coordination lost → everything fails.
