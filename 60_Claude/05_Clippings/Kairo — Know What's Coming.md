## Meta Just Open-Sourced a Digital Copy of the Human Brain

A foundation model that predicts human brain activity from video, audio, and text. Open-sourced for free. Here is what it means for the future of AI.

## Situation

Meta just open-sourced TRIBE v2 — a foundation model that predicts how the human brain responds to anything it sees, hears, or reads. They scanned 720 people using fMRI while they watched movies, listened to podcasts, and read text. Over 1,000 hours of brain data. Then they trained an AI to predict exactly what happens inside a human brain when it processes the real world.

The model predicts whole-brain activity across 70,000 voxels — a 70-fold increase in resolution compared to anything that existed before. And it works zero-shot: you can give it a new person it has never scanned, play them a video, and it will predict their brain response without any calibration.

Meta released the model weights on [Hugging Face](https://huggingface.co/facebook/tribev2), the full codebase on [GitHub](https://github.com/facebookresearch/tribev2), and an [interactive demo](https://aidemos.atmeta.com/tribev2/). The entire thing is open-source under a Creative Commons license. Anyone can run it right now.

## Analysis

This is the same pattern we saw with the Transformer paper. In 2017, Google published "Attention Is All You Need" — a research paper that nobody outside of AI paid attention to. They gave it away for free. That one paper built ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini. A trillion-dollar industry came from one open-sourced paper.

TRIBE v2 has that same energy. It is foundational research that enables an entirely new category. The model uses Meta's own AI stack — LLaMA 3.2 for text, V-JEPA2 for video, and Wav2Vec-BERT for audio — unified through a Transformer architecture that maps multimodal representations directly onto the cortical surface of the brain.

Whoever takes this model and puts it inside a wearable device — a device that sees what you see, hears what you hear — that device will not just record your life. It will understand how your brain processes it. This is the bridge between capturing data and understanding cognition.

## Future Outlook

We are at the beginning of cognitive AI. Not artificial general intelligence — something more specific and arguably more consequential. AI that does not just process language or generate images, but understands how the human brain processes reality itself.

Within 18 months, expect to see TRIBE v2 integrated into wearable prototypes. Devices that can predict your cognitive state — what you are paying attention to, how deeply you are processing it, whether you are engaged or drifting. This changes education, therapy, content creation, and product design.

The fact that Meta open-sourced this is the critical detail. Closed models create monopolies. Open models create ecosystems. The Transformer paper created a $2 trillion ecosystem precisely because anyone could build on it. TRIBE v2 has the same structural conditions. The question is not whether something massive gets built on this. The question is who builds it first.
