## The One Idea That Makes This Click

The old web was humans searching, reading, comparing, clicking, and buying. Your beautiful website existed to win a *person's* attention.

The new web is agents discovering, evaluating, paying, and renewing. The customer isn't a person anymore. It's the AI working on a person's behalf.

Here's the line to tattoo on your brain:

A human customer wants persuasion. An agent customer wants structured capability, permission, and trust.

Your slick landing page, your clever headline, your hero video? An agent doesn't care. It reads your data, checks whether it can actually *do* something with you (book, buy, return, schedule), confirms it can trust you, and moves on. If it can't understand you or act on your site, you're invisible to it. Not low-ranked. Invisible.

That single reframe is the whole game. Keep it in your head for all four phases.

Phase 1

## Understand the New Internet

Goal: actually believe this is real, with numbers, so you act instead of waiting.

Most people hear "AI agents are the new customer" and file it under sci-fi. Then they see the data and go quiet.

Bots already make up **51% of all internet traffic** (Imperva's 2025 report). That's the first time in history machines outnumber humans online. Not a prediction. Right now.

Agentic traffic specifically jumped **1,300% from January to August 2025** (HUMAN Security). It's not creeping, it's compounding. AI traffic to retail sites is up **4,700% year over year** (Adobe), and **23% of Americans bought something through AI in the past month** (Morgan Stanley).

Morgan Stanley projects agent shoppers will drive **$190 to $385 billion in U.S. online spending by 2030.** That's the "$100 billion up for grabs" idea, sourced and then some.

Phase 2

## AEO: The New SEO

Goal: get recommended by the AI, not just ranked by Google.

For 20 years the game was SEO: rank high so humans click you. That game is dying in real time.

Gartner expects traditional **search volume to drop 25% by 2026.** Google's AI Overviews are already linked to a **25% drop in publisher referral traffic** (Digiday). People get the answer and never click. McKinsey projects **$750 billion in U.S. revenue will flow through AI search by 2028,** with around half of consumers already using it to make buying decisions.

The new game is **AEO: Answer Engine Optimization.** Instead of optimizing to rank for a human, you optimize to be the answer an AI cites, trusts, and recommends. When someone asks ChatGPT "what's the best \[your thing\]," you want to be the name it says.

The good news: a lot of AEO is stuff you can actually control. Clear answers to real questions. Structured content. Getting mentioned in the places AI trusts. This is the best practical breakdown I've found, from the founder of an SEO company that does this for a living, not a hype channel.

Phase 3

## Build the Actions

Goal: let an agent actually do something on your site, not just read it.

Here's a stat that should change how you think about your website: agents already spend **87% of their visits on product pages, but only 2.2% ever reach a cart or checkout** (HUMAN Security). They show up ready to act and hit a wall, because your site was built for a human to click through, not for an agent to transact.

So the shift is: stop building things only humans can use, start building things an agent can *invoke*. Instead of a contact form, an action the agent can call. Instead of support docs, executable support where the agent does the refund, the return, the reschedule. Instead of a landing page full of slogans, a clear statement of what an agent can actually *do* with you.

The technology that makes this possible is called **MCP (Model Context Protocol).** Don't let the name scare you. MCP is just a standard way to give an AI a set of buttons it's allowed to push, like a universal adapter between an agent and your business. Anthropic (the company behind Claude) created it, and it's now the default standard everyone uses. This is the clearest explanation, straight from the source.

Phase 4

## The Plumbing: Trust & Payments

Goal: understand how agents pay and prove who they are, because that's where the giants are betting.

An agent buying for you needs things a human never did: an identity (who is it acting for?), a wallet (what can it spend, who approves it?), and a receipt (what did it actually do?). Whoever builds that trust layer owns the agent economy, and the biggest names on earth already know it.

In 2025 alone: **Mastercard** launched Agent Pay and **Visa** launched Intelligent Commerce, both built to let agents pay safely. **Google** released AP2, an open payment standard backed by 60+ partners. And **OpenAI and Stripe** shipped Instant Checkout inside ChatGPT, so you can buy without leaving the chat.

And it's already moving real money: Adobe measured an **805% jump in AI traffic to retail on Black Friday 2025,** and Salesforce says AI influenced **$3 billion** of Black Friday sales.

The opportunity

## Where the $100 Billion Actually Is

Greg's whole point: nearly every tool you use will get rebuilt for agents, and almost nobody is building those versions yet. It's 1999 again and the lots are unclaimed.

Here's the cheat code. Take any popular software or service and ask: **what's the agent-native version of this?** That question alone is a startup idea generator. A few he floated that anyone can run with:

Making businesses **agent-ready** (the new "I'll do your SEO," wide open)

**Agent identity & permissions** (proving who an agent works for)

**Agent-readable docs & pricing pages** as a service

**MCP servers** for whole categories (franchises, SaaS, local industries)

**Agent support desks** and **audit trails**

And the reason this is urgent: **only 22% of businesses** say they're actually ready for agents (Microsoft). The gap between "agents are here" and "businesses are ready" *is* the opportunity. You don't need to be technical to fill it. You need to be early and useful.
