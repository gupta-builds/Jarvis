# Career Strategy

> Distilled from mentorship planning, internship search notes, and career progress tracking

## Deep Dive

### One-Sentence Version

Career strategy for a CS student means converting coursework and projects into demonstrable capability through a prioritized pipeline: ship projects → build portfolio → practice interviews → network through warm intros.

### What It Is

A structured approach to career development with four parallel tracks:
1. **Project execution** — ship real projects that demonstrate end-to-end capability (not just tutorials)
2. **Portfolio conversion** — turn every project into a portfolio artifact with quantified impact
3. **Interview readiness** — practice technical and behavioral interviews with real stories
4. **Network building** — use mentorship, informational interviews, and warm intros to build a pipeline

### Why It Matters

The difference between a student who gets internship offers and one who doesn't is rarely raw technical skill. It is the ability to demonstrate that skill through shipped projects, practiced stories, and warm introductions. Career strategy is the system that converts learning into opportunities.

### Real Example

Summer planning uses a three-track strategy:
- **Track 1 (if internship)**: onboard fast, ship one feature end-to-end, convert to portfolio piece, secure return offer intel
- **Track 2 (if no internship)**: deep work on Learning Tracker (5 sprints), BOOM UROP extension, interview prep in August
- **Track 3 (side track)**: startup validation — problem thesis, Figma prototype, MVP, 5-user pilot

Decision framework uses MoSCoW:
- **Must-haves**: one shipped project, portfolio live with 3 projects, resume updated, 2 mock interviews
- **Should-haves**: blog post about BOOM work, 10 informational interviews, consistent GitHub commits
- **Could-haves**: startup validation, open source contribution
- **Won't-haves (defer)**: multi-platform mobile app, monetization

### Contrast With

- **Career strategy vs job search**: Job search is reactive (apply to postings). Career strategy is proactive (build capability, then convert it into opportunities).
- **Depth vs breadth early career**: Depth in one area (full-stack + AI systems) creates a clear story. Breadth across too many areas creates a confusing resume. Decide: "Am I a full-stack engineer who uses AI, or an AI engineer who can build full-stack?"
- **OPT/CPT planning vs ignoring timelines**: International students have hard deadlines. OPT timeline planning must start 6+ months before graduation. Ignoring this turns a career strategy into an emergency.
