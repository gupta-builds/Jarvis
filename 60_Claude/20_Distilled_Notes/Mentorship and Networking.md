# Mentorship and Networking

> Distilled from [[20_Progress/Mentorship Program/Plan]] and mentorship program materials

## Deep Dive

### One-Sentence Version

Mentorship is a relationship-building vehicle where you lead by demonstrating execution capability, not a transactional exchange for job referrals.

### What It Is

The CSE Mentor Program matches students with industry professionals. The student's job is to:
- Set goals and lead the relationship
- Contact mentor at least twice per month
- Bring demos, progress updates, and specific questions
- Act on advice and report back

The mentor provides career advice, guidance, and networking opportunities — not jobs or internships directly.

### Why It Matters

Warm introductions convert at 10-50x the rate of cold applications. A mentor who has seen you execute over 6 months will advocate for you in ways a resume cannot. The mentorship program is the fastest path to building a professional network while still in school.

### Real Example

Meeting structure that works with mentor Ahnaf (Best Buy engineer):
- **Format**: 30-minute Zoom, every 2 weeks
- **Structure**: Demo → Shipped/Blocked/Deciding → Ask
- **Bring**: Updated "Role Brief" each time (rotate through Frontend, Backend, Full-stack, DevOps, Data, Mobile)
- **Outcomes to secure**: code review of one complex component, introduction to 1-2 engineers, mock interview slot

The MoSCoW framework (Must-have, Should-have, Could-have, Won't-have) from the mentorship planning applies to prioritizing summer work: ship one project and complete 2 mock interviews are Must-haves. Open source contributions are Could-haves.

### Contrast With

- **Mentorship vs informational interviews**: Mentorship is ongoing (6+ months). Informational interviews are one-time conversations. Both build network, but mentorship builds trust through demonstrated execution.
- **Asking for a job vs demonstrating capability**: Asking for a job is transactional and explicitly not the program's purpose. Demonstrating execution over time naturally leads to referrals and advocacy.
- **Networking quantity vs quality**: 100 LinkedIn connections mean nothing. 5 people who have seen your work and can vouch for your execution are worth more.

## Diagnostic Questions

- What are you bringing to your next mentor meeting? (Demo, progress update, specific question)
- Can your mentor describe one technical decision you made? If not, you haven't shown enough work.
- How many warm introductions have you secured through the mentorship so far?
