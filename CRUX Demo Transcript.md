# CRUX — 1-Minute Demo Transcript

*Spoken over a screen recording. ~60s. Pause where marked. Let the screen do the work — don't rush the two-points-lost moment.*

---

**[0:00 — landing screen on screen]**
"Quick one. Every day, you fire off a message like this — a few real things, buried in one line."

**[0:07 — scroll to the demo, raw message visible]**
"Three asks in here. Watch what happens to it the normal way."

**[0:11 — click Send. Left column inflates]**
"AI blows your one line up into *this* — a polished email you never actually wrote. And nobody's reading all that… so on the other end, their AI crushes it back down to a bullet."

**[0:25 — the two dropped points get marked red]**
"And look — two of your three points just disappeared. The meeting moved, sure. But legal signing off? The heads-up for Sara? Gone. Nobody lied. The point just *died in the round-trip*."

**[0:36 — right column: the structured intent card]**
"Now the same message through CRUX. It doesn't send words — it carries your *intent*. The ask, the FYI, the deadline — structured, intact. Three of three kept."

**[0:47 — click the readback confirm]**
"And this is the human part: it doesn't assume. It asks the person on the other side — *did I get this right?* They confirm. If it's off, they fix it — and the sender's meaning wins. The loop closes."

**[0:57 — final state on screen]**
"Same message. One side guesses. One side knows. That's CRUX — the point survives the trip."

---

### Delivery notes
- **Hook fast.** The first 10 seconds decide if they lean in. Lead with "you," not "we."
- **Slow down at 0:25.** The "two points lost" reveal is the whole pitch — let it land silently for a beat.
- **Land the closer clean.** "One side guesses. One side knows." — then stop. Don't add anything after the tagline.
- Total spoken approx 150 words. If you're rushing, cut the 0:07 line, not the 0:25 beat.
